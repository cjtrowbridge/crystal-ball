What We're Working On

Member Survey

We have been tasked with surveying the Comfort & Joy community.

Methodology

The first thing we did was to categorize everyone who is in the organization or adjacent to the organization into groups which can be researched.

As you can see, informal membership groups are listed to the left, while formal membership groups are listed to the right. We also separate current formal members from past formal members. Each population has an ID number as shown.

One fundamental premise in population research is that you should not poll everyone. You should instead poll a random sample of around 5% of the population in order to get a more accurate result. People may have bought tickets for friends, or may have used multiple email addresses. We don't want to overvalue or undervalue any certain kind of person, so a random sample provides a more accurate result versus polling everyone.

If each of these populations is sampled at a rate of 5%, that means we have 20 cohorts for each population, or twenty subsets of 5% from each population which together make up 100% of that population. These are identified by letters. So for example, the first cohort from population 1 is "Cohort 1A."

The first round of surveys will target populations 1,2, and 3. Later testing will expand to poll all the populations, and eventually track changes in populations as people move from group to group.

Testing

We have completed an initial draft survey. This will be submitted to the purple circle and the board at the next meeting on 3/12/20.

We hope to have results for Cohorts 1A, 2A, and 3A for this initial version of the survey completed shortly thereafter, and then make any last adjustments before rolling out the finalized survey.
